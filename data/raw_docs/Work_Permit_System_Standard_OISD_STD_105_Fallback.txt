OISD STANDARD 105: WORK PERMIT SYSTEM STANDARD (FALLBACK REFERENCE)

1. SCOPE & GENERAL REQUIREMENT
The Work Permit System is a formal written system used to control certain types of non-routine work that are identified as potentially hazardous. It is an essential part of safe systems of work. No work shall be carried out in an industrial facility (refinery, steel plant, chemical plant) without a valid Work Permit issued by an authorized receiver/issuer.

2. TYPES OF PERMITS
2.1 Hot Work Permit: Required for any work involving spark, flame, heat, or temperature elevation capable of igniting flammable materials. Example: welding, grinding, cutting, open fire, or use of non-explosion-proof electrical equipment.
2.2 Cold Work Permit: Required for maintenance activities that do not generate heat or spark but still involve safety hazards.
2.3 Confined Space Entry Permit: Required for entering vessels, tanks, columns, sewers, or pits where hazardous atmospheres or entrapment risks exist.
2.4 Electrical Work Permit: Required for work on high/medium voltage installations, requiring lockout-tagout (LOTO).

3. SECTION 4: HOT WORK PERMIT SYSTEM AND SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
3.1 Section 4.1 (Gas Testing Requirement): Before issuing a Hot Work Permit, the area must be tested for flammable gas concentration using a calibrated gas detector.
3.2 Section 4.2 (Permitted Flammable Gas Limits): No hot work shall be permitted if the concentration of flammable gas in the atmosphere exceeds 1% by volume or 10% LEL.
3.3 Section 4.3 (Critical Gas Limits and Immediate Stop): If the flammable gas concentration rises above 20% LEL, all hot work permits in that zone are immediately suspended. Hot work is strictly prohibited in the presence of gas above 25% LEL. Under no circumstances should hot work occur when gas reaches 35% LEL or higher. This condition represents an immediate explosion hazard.
3.4 Section 4.4 (Fire Watch): A designated fire watch with appropriate fire-extinguishing equipment must be stationed at the hot work site for the duration of the work.

4. SECTION 6: CONFINED SPACE ENTRY REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Section 6.1 (Oxygen Concentration): The oxygen concentration in a confined space must be checked prior to entry and monitored continuously. Safe entry levels must be between 19.5% and 23.5% by volume. Entry is strictly prohibited if oxygen levels fall below 19.5% without self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA).
4.2 Section 6.2 (Toxic Gases): Toxic gas concentrations must be below safe limits (H2S < 10 ppm, CO < 30 ppm, Methane/Methane equivalents < 5% LEL).
4.3 Section 6.3 (Continuous Ventilation): Continuous mechanical ventilation (forced draft fan) is mandatory during the entire duration of the confined space entry.
4.4 Section 6.4 (Standby Person): A standby watch must be stationed at the entry point of the confined space to monitor the workers inside and coordinate emergency rescue.

5. SECTION 7: SPATIAL SAFETY DISTANCES & SIMULTANEOUS OPERATIONS (SIMOPS)
5.1 Section 7.3.1 (Clearance Distances): Hot work must maintain a clearance distance of at least 15 meters from any potential hydrocarbon source, drain, or venting point.
5.2 Section 7.3.2 (Hot Work Prohibitions): Section 7.3.2 prohibits hot work within 10 meters of a gas source or active gas leakage. Issuing overlapping permits (such as hot work adjacent to confined space venting or vessel draining) is strictly prohibited.
5.3 Section 7.4 (SIMOPS): Simultaneous operations (maintenance and operations) in overlapping zones must undergo a rigorous risk assessment.

6. SECTION 10: EMERGENCY ACTIONS AND PERMIT SUSPENSION
6.1 Section 10.1 (Alarms): In the event of a gas leak or fire alarm, all work permits in the facility are immediately suspended. Workers must safe-state their equipment and evacuate to designated assembly points.
6.2 Section 10.2 (Permit Revocation): The safety officer or plant manager has the authority to revoke any permit if abnormal process conditions (gas drift, pressure spike, temperature deviation) are detected.
